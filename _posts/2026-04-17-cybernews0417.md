## Two-Factor Authentication Expands Beyond Traditional Devices

Two-factor authentication (2FA) is evolving beyond traditional desktop and mobile devices, with new methods emerging to improve both security and usability. Innovations include hardware-based authentication, biometric verification, and passkey-based approaches that reduce reliance on passwords. These advancements aim to address common weaknesses such as phishing and credential theft. However, expanding 2FA beyond familiar platforms also introduces challenges in deployment and user adoption. Organizations must balance stronger security with usability when implementing next-generation authentication.

## Mirax Malware Campaign Compromises 220,000 Accounts

The Mirax malware campaign has reportedly compromised over 220,000 accounts, giving attackers full remote control over infected systems. The malware enables attackers to execute commands, steal data, and maintain persistent access. Its widespread impact highlights the scale and effectiveness of modern malware campaigns. Victims may be unaware their systems are compromised due to stealthy operation. Security experts recommend endpoint monitoring and timely patching to mitigate risk.  
[Read More](https://securityaffairs.com/190842/uncategorized/mirax-malware-campaign-hits-220k-accounts-enables-full-remote-control.html)

## UAC-0247 Campaign Targets Browser and WhatsApp Data

A threat campaign tracked as UAC-0247 is actively stealing browser data and WhatsApp information from victims. The attackers use phishing and malicious payloads to gain access to sensitive user data. Once compromised, credentials and communication data can be exfiltrated. This campaign highlights the growing focus on messaging platforms and personal data. Users are advised to be cautious with downloads and enable strong security controls.  
[Read More](https://cybersecuritynews.com/new-uac-0247-campaign-steals-browser-and-whatsapp-data/)

## Cisco ISE Vulnerabilities Pose Enterprise Risk

Multiple vulnerabilities have been discovered in Cisco Identity Services Engine (ISE), a critical component for network access control. These flaws could allow attackers to bypass authentication or gain unauthorized access. Given ISE’s role in enterprise environments, exploitation could have widespread consequences. Cisco has released patches to address the issues. Organizations should update systems immediately and review access controls.  
[Read More](https://cybersecuritynews.com/cisco-ise-vulnerabilities/)

## U.S. Nationals Sentenced in North Korean IT Worker Scheme

Two U.S. nationals have been sentenced for their involvement in a scheme supporting North Korean IT workers operating remote laptop farms. These operations allowed North Korean actors to secure jobs in foreign companies and funnel earnings back to the regime. The scheme also posed security risks by granting unauthorized access to corporate systems. Authorities highlight this as a growing insider threat vector. Organizations are urged to strengthen hiring and access verification processes.

Security researchers have mapped over 1,250 command-and-control (C2) servers linked to Russian hosting infrastructure. These servers are used to coordinate malware campaigns and manage compromised systems. The findings provide valuable insights into attacker infrastructure and operations. Disrupting such networks can significantly impact cybercriminal activity. Organizations can use this intelligence to improve threat detection and response.
