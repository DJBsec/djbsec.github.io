## 1. Palo Alto PAN-OS Firewall Vulnerability Exposes Enterprise Networks

A critical vulnerability in **Palo Alto Networks' PAN-OS** has been discovered, potentially allowing unauthenticated remote attackers to bypass security controls. This flaw affects certain versions with GlobalProtect enabled, which could lead to full device compromise. Palo Alto has released patches and urges immediate updates across impacted deployments. This vulnerability poses significant risk to enterprises relying on Palo Alto firewalls for perimeter defense.

[Read more](https://cybersecuritynews.com/palo-alto-pan-os-firewall-vulnerability/)

## 2. Zero-Day Exploits Hit Cisco ISE and Citrix in Coordinated Campaign

A sophisticated threat campaign is actively exploiting **zero-day vulnerabilities** in **Cisco Identity Services Engine (ISE)** and **Citrix NetScaler systems**. Security researchers say the attacks are linked to a well-resourced APT group leveraging both zero-days in tandem for lateral movement and data exfiltration. Cisco and Citrix have released advisories, and patches are either available or in development. Organizations are urged to monitor for signs of compromise and apply mitigations immediately.

[Read more](https://www.csoonline.com/article/4089200/zero-day-exploits-hit-cisco-ise-and-citrix-systems-in-an-advanced-campaign.html)

## 3. Microsoft Defender for Office 365 Gets Screenshot Monitoring Alerts

Microsoft has added a new feature to **Defender for Office 365** that alerts administrators when users take screenshots of sensitive content. The feature is part of a broader push to prevent data leakage through visual capture methods. It works by integrating with existing DLP policies and logs screenshot behavior tied to high-risk content. This helps organizations monitor insider threats and enforce stricter content controls.

[Read more](https://cybersecuritynews.com/microsoft-defendero365-new-feature/)

## 4. Microsoft Teams Rolls Out Screen Capture Prevention Feature

Microsoft is rolling out a **screen capture prevention** feature for Teams, designed to protect sensitive content from being copied or recorded. The feature applies to Teams meetings and chats classified under strict compliance or confidentiality policies. It's currently being deployed to GCC tenants with plans for commercial rollouts soon. This enhancement aligns with Microsoft’s Zero Trust strategy for secure collaboration.

[Read more](https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/microsoft/microsoft-rolls-out-screen-capture-prevention-for-teams-users/)

## 5. Steganography Used to Deliver XWorm Malware via PNG Files

Security researchers have uncovered a new campaign using **steganography** to embed **XWorm malware** in seemingly harmless PNG image files. The malware is hidden within image metadata and executed via malicious scripts that extract and run the payload. This method allows attackers to evade antivirus detection by masking threats in common media files. Organizations are urged to inspect unusual image downloads and apply behavioral detection rules.

[Read more](https://cybersecuritynews.com/steganography-attacks-xworm-in-pngs/)

## 6. RCE Flaw in ImunifyAV Puts Millions of Linux-Hosted Websites at Risk

A critical **remote code execution vulnerability** has been discovered in **ImunifyAV**, a popular Linux antivirus tool used by web hosting providers. The flaw could allow attackers to execute arbitrary commands and compromise entire servers hosting thousands of sites. With a public proof-of-concept already released, the risk of mass exploitation is high. Users are urged to patch immediately and monitor web server activity.

[Read more](https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/rce-flaw-in-imunifyav-puts-millions-of-linux-hosted-sites-at-risk/)

## 7. FBI & CISA Issue Joint Advisory on Akira Ransomware Surge

The **FBI and CISA** have issued a joint advisory on the rise of **Akira ransomware**, warning that the group is expanding its attacks across sectors including manufacturing, education, and healthcare. The advisory outlines IOCs, TTPs, and mitigation steps to help organizations defend against current campaigns. Akira actors are known to exfiltrate data before encryption and threaten public leaks to pressure victims. Agencies recommend strict MFA, patching, and network segmentation.

[Read more](https://cyberscoop.com/akira-ransomware-fbi-cisa-joint-advisory/)

## 8. Akira Linux Variant Now Targets Nutanix Virtual Machines

Akira ransomware operators have developed a **Linux encryptor variant** that specifically targets **Nutanix virtual machines**. This expands the group's reach into hybrid and cloud environments, posing new challenges for enterprise backup and recovery operations. The attackers use SSH access to deploy the malware and disrupt critical infrastructure. CISA urges immediate action for any organization using Nutanix in their data centers.

[Read more](https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/cisa-warns-of-akira-ransomware-linux-encryptor-targeting-nutanix-vms/)

## 9. Chinese APT Targets Claude AI Users in Espionage Campaign

A Chinese APT group has reportedly launched a cyberespionage campaign targeting users of **Claude**, an AI chatbot alternative to ChatGPT. Attackers are distributing trojanized versions of Claude clients or phishing login pages to steal API tokens and internal queries. The stolen data could expose sensitive intellectual property or internal strategies from enterprise users. Analysts warn that AI usage creates new supply chain attack surfaces.

[Read more](https://go.theregister.com/feed/www.theregister.com/2025/11/13/chinese_spies_claude_attacks/)

## 10. FortiWeb Vulnerability Exploited to Create Admin Accounts

A critical vulnerability in **Fortinet’s FortiWeb** web application firewall is being **actively exploited** to create rogue admin accounts. A public proof-of-concept exploit has been released, and threat actors are already abusing it to gain persistent control of affected appliances. Fortinet has issued patches, and unpatched systems are urged to restrict access immediately. The flaw allows attackers to bypass authentication and gain full control.

[Read more](https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/fortiweb-flaw-with-public-poc-actively-exploited-to-create-admin-users/)

## 11. Checkout.com Suffers Breach; Payment Data Feared Compromised

**Checkout.com**, a major global payment processor, has confirmed a data breach that may have exposed payment data and merchant credentials. The incident is under investigation, but early signs point to unauthorized access via a compromised API key. Security experts are concerned about downstream impacts to e-commerce clients using the platform. Checkout.com has advised clients to rotate API credentials and monitor for fraudulent activity.

[Read more](https://cybersecuritynews.com/checkout-com-hacked/)

## 12. Kraken Ransomware Benchmarks Systems to Optimize Encryption

**Kraken ransomware** has introduced a novel feature: **system benchmarking** to optimize encryption performance based on hardware specs. Before encrypting files, the malware runs diagnostic tests to determine the fastest encryption strategy for the victim's system. This allows attackers to reduce downtime and increase the likelihood of full encryption before detection. Organizations are advised to monitor for unusual benchmarking or stress-testing processes on endpoints.

[Read more](https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/kraken-ransomware-benchmarks-systems-for-optimal-encryption-choice/)
